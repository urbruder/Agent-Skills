# SEO Brief Framework

## Search Intent Types

- Informational: user wants to learn, compare concepts, or solve a problem.
- Commercial: user is evaluating options, vendors, tools, or approaches.
- Transactional: user is close to purchase or signup.
- Navigational: user wants a specific brand, page, or entity.
- Local: user wants location-specific services or businesses.

## Differentiation Prompts

Use these to avoid generic briefs:

- What does the target reader need that competitors may ignore?
- What decision does this article help the reader make?
- What examples, frameworks, templates, or checklists would make the page more useful?
- What misconceptions should the article correct?
- What business outcome should the article connect to?

## QA Checklist

A strong brief should include:

- Clear intent match
- Specific reader and problem
- Writer-ready outline
- Unique angle
- Practical examples or proof guidance
- FAQs based on likely objections or follow-up questions
- CTA aligned with funnel stage
- Natural keyword usage guidance
