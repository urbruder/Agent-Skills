# Voice Profile Framework

Use this reference for deeper voice analysis and reusable voice systems.

## Voice dimensions

### Tone
Examples: direct, warm, analytical, skeptical, witty, calm, bold, premium, plainspoken, tactical, reflective.

### Energy
Low, medium, high, urgent, calm, intense, conversational, polished.

### Formality
Casual, business casual, executive, technical, academic, salesy, editorial.

### Sentence rhythm
Look for:
- short punchy lines
- long layered sentences
- fragments
- repetition
- one-line paragraphs
- parentheticals
- em dashes
- rhetorical questions
- numbered lists

### Vocabulary
Track:
- recurring verbs
- category language
- industry terms
- taboo words
- preferred metaphors
- level of jargon
- simplicity versus sophistication

### Point of view
Identify whether the voice:
- teaches
- challenges
- confesses
- narrates
- advises
- sells
- debates
- simplifies
- diagnoses

## Ethical boundaries

Do not help impersonate someone without authorization. For public figures or third-party individuals, offer a style-inspired alternative based on high-level traits rather than direct imitation.

## Sample strength

- 1 sample: provisional profile
- 3-5 samples: usable voice profile
- 10+ samples across formats: strong reusable system

## Common business voice archetypes

### Founder operator
Direct, practical, learned-from-experience, skeptical of theory.

### Expert advisor
Clear, structured, authoritative, generous with frameworks.

### Premium brand
Minimal, confident, polished, restrained, low hype.

### Challenger brand
Contrarian, sharp, category-critical, opinionated.

### Friendly educator
Warm, simple, encouraging, practical, accessible.
