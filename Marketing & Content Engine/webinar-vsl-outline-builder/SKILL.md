# Webinar/VSL Outline Builder

Build a high-converting webinar or VSL flow from an offer.

## Core workflow

When the user provides an offer, product, service, course, event, sales page, lead magnet, audience, or rough idea:

1. Extract the conversion inputs:
   - audience and buyer stage
   - offer and price point if provided
   - promised outcome
   - core problem
   - old way versus new way
   - mechanism or method
   - proof or credibility
   - objections
   - desired CTA
   - webinar/VSL length
   - traffic source or audience temperature

2. If information is missing, make practical assumptions and label them briefly.

3. Choose the best flow:
   - **Webinar/masterclass:** more teaching, trust-building, and section breaks.
   - **VSL:** tighter pacing, more emotional momentum, fewer tangents.
   - **Demo webinar:** product proof, use cases, and implementation path.
   - **Challenge/workshop:** quick wins, habit shift, and offer bridge.

4. Produce a complete outline from opening hook to final CTA.

5. Use `references/webinar-vsl-flow-framework.md` for longer presentations, detailed slide maps, offer transitions, or advanced objection handling.

## Conversion principles

A strong webinar or VSL should:

- create a belief shift before pitching
- teach enough to build trust but not overwhelm
- make the offer the next logical step
- address objections before they become exits
- use proof ethically
- avoid fake scarcity or exaggerated promises
- keep the viewer oriented at every stage

## Length guidance

### 5-10 minute VSL
Use one big problem, one mechanism, one proof point, one CTA.

### 20-30 minute VSL
Use problem, reframe, mechanism, 2-3 teaching points, proof, offer, objections, CTA.

### 45-60 minute webinar
Use opening, authority, problem, stakes, 3-part teaching framework, proof, offer stack, bonuses if legitimate, objections, FAQ, CTA.

### 90-minute workshop
Use teaching, live examples, exercises, Q&A, offer bridge, CTA.

## Offer transition rules

The pitch should not feel like a hard turn. Bridge from teaching to offer with:

- "Now that you know what has to happen, the question is how fast you want to implement it."
- "You can do this manually, or you can use the system we built to make it easier."
- "If you want help applying this to your business, here is the next step."

## Guardrails

Do not:

- invent income claims, testimonials, urgency, bonuses, or guarantees
- manipulate fear or shame
- imply certain outcomes without proof
- overload the outline with too many ideas
- make the webinar only a disguised sales pitch

If proof is missing, include proof placeholders instead of inventing specifics.

## Final quality check

Before responding, verify that:

- the flow creates a clear belief shift
- the viewer receives useful value before the pitch
- the offer appears naturally connected to the teaching
- objections are addressed credibly
- the CTA is specific
- claims are supported or cautious
- the outline is usable for slides, video scripting, or a live presentation
