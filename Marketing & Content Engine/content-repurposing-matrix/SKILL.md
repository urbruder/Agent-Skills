# Content Repurposing Matrix

Turn one idea into a week of platform-native content across seven channels.

## Core workflow

When the user provides an idea, article, transcript, video, podcast, newsletter, offer, case study, or rough insight:

1. Extract the source idea:
   - core message
   - target audience
   - business goal
   - key takeaway
   - proof or example
   - offer or CTA
   - audience awareness level
   - preferred platforms

2. If the user does not specify platforms, default to:
   - LinkedIn
   - X
   - Instagram
   - Threads
   - TikTok/Reels
   - YouTube Shorts
   - Email/newsletter

3. Atomize the idea into platform-native content. Do not simply paste the same copy everywhere.

4. Maintain one strategic message while adapting format, pacing, hook style, and CTA per platform.

5. Use `references/repurposing-platform-framework.md` when the user asks for more platforms, weekly calendars, content pillars, or campaign mapping.

## Repurposing principles

Good repurposing is not duplication. It should:

- preserve the core idea
- adapt to platform behavior
- vary the hook
- vary the format
- match reader intent
- avoid audience fatigue
- connect back to a business goal
- create multiple entry points into the same message

## Content formats to use

Choose formats based on the idea:

- text post
- carousel outline
- short video script
- thread
- list post
- contrarian take
- story post
- how-to post
- quote graphic
- email opener
- poll
- mini case study
- checklist
- FAQ post
- founder reflection
- objection-handling post

## Platform guidance

### LinkedIn
Use professional insight, authority, stories, frameworks, or practical advice. Tie to business outcomes.

### X
Use concise takes, threads, sharp observations, lists, and punchy one-liners.

### Instagram
Use carousels, reels, visual hooks, concise captions, and save-worthy advice.

### Threads
Use conversational, fast, human posts with simple hooks and low-friction engagement.

### TikTok/Reels
Use a strong first 3 seconds, direct spoken lines, visual movement, and one clear idea.

### YouTube Shorts
Use searchable or curiosity-led hooks, quick structure, and a clear payoff.

### Email/Newsletter
Use a stronger narrative or teaching arc. Add context, story, and a meaningful CTA.

## Business priority

Each matrix should serve one business goal:

- build authority
- drive leads
- nurture prospects
- promote an offer
- support a launch
- educate customers
- handle objections
- drive event registration
- revive old content
- amplify a case study

## Final quality check

Before responding, verify that:

- all seven platform outputs are distinct
- the core message remains consistent
- each platform draft feels native
- hooks are not repetitive
- CTAs align with the campaign goal
- no proof or claims are invented
- the output is ready for a marketer to schedule or refine
