# Landing Page Framework

Use this reference for deeper landing page strategy, hero variants, CRO review, or long-form sales pages.

## Page structure by offer type

### Lead magnet
Use a short page:
1. Hero
2. What you get
3. Why it matters
4. Who it is for
5. CTA

### Consultation or call booking
Use a medium page:
1. Hero
2. Problem
3. Outcome
4. Why the process works
5. Proof
6. Who it is for
7. What happens on the call
8. FAQ
9. CTA

### SaaS or product
Use a medium page:
1. Hero
2. Pain
3. Product as solution
4. Key features translated into benefits
5. Use cases
6. Proof
7. Integrations or workflow fit
8. Pricing or trial CTA
9. FAQ
10. CTA

### High-ticket service
Use a longer page:
1. Hero
2. Problem and cost of inaction
3. Point of view
4. Method or mechanism
5. Offer details
6. Proof
7. Process
8. Who it is for and not for
9. Objections
10. CTA

## Hero formulas

### Outcome without tradeoff
[Audience] get [outcome] without [painful tradeoff].

### Mechanism-led
The [mechanism] for [audience] who need [outcome].

### Problem-aware
Still [painful problem]? [Offer] helps you [better path].

### Category plus differentiator
[Category] built for [specific audience/use case].

### Speed to value
Go from [current state] to [better state] in [credible timeframe or process].

Use timeframes only when provided or clearly safe.

## Copy ingredients

### Promise
The result the visitor wants.

### Mechanism
How the offer creates that result.

### Proof
Why the visitor should believe it.

### Friction reducer
Why acting now feels safe, easy, or worthwhile.

### CTA
The next step.

## Awareness levels

### Unaware
Educate around the problem before introducing the offer.

### Problem-aware
Lead with pain and cost of inaction.

### Solution-aware
Lead with why this solution is better.

### Product-aware
Lead with differentiators, proof, offer, and CTA.

### Most-aware
Lead with direct offer and urgency or incentive if legitimate.

## CTA guidance

Good CTA copy completes the phrase "I want to..."

Examples:
- Get the free checklist
- Book my consultation
- See the platform
- Start my free trial
- Request the proposal
- Join the waitlist

Avoid vague CTAs:
- Submit
- Learn more
- Click here
- Get started, unless context makes it specific

## Proof placeholders

If proof is missing, suggest specific proof assets:
- customer quote
- before and after metric
- client logo
- case study result
- founder credential
- product screenshot
- demo clip
- security or compliance badge
- process guarantee
- third-party review

Do not invent proof.

## CRO review checklist

Check for:

- unclear audience
- vague promise
- weak first-screen CTA
- missing mechanism
- missing proof
- too many competing actions
- feature-heavy copy
- jargon
- unsupported claims
- weak objection handling
- unclear offer details
- poor page flow
