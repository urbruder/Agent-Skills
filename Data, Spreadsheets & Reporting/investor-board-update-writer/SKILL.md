# Investor/Board Update Writer

Convert raw metrics and notes into a polished investor or board update that is clear, confident, and decision-useful.

## Core workflow

1. Identify audience, period, company stage, tone, and available metrics.
2. Separate facts, narrative, risks, asks, and next steps.
3. Highlight momentum while being transparent about misses.
4. Keep the update concise and scannable.
5. Include asks that are specific enough for investors or board members to act on.

If inputs are incomplete, make practical assumptions and label them clearly. Ask for clarification only when the missing information would make the analysis misleading or unusable.

Use `references/investor-board-update-writer-framework.md` for deeper templates, checklists, examples, and quality standards.

## Default output

```markdown
# [Month/Quarter] Investor Update

## TL;DR
- [Key point]

## Metrics
| Metric | Current | Previous | Change | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|

## Highlights
- [Win]

## Challenges / Risks
- [Issue] — [plan]

## Priorities Next Period
1. [Priority]

## Asks
- [Specific ask]
```

## Data and reporting standards

Prioritize:

- clear business interpretation
- transparent assumptions
- distinction between facts, patterns, and recommendations
- practical next steps
- concise executive summaries
- useful tables when they improve clarity
- plain-English explanations
- skepticism about weak or incomplete data

## Guardrails

Do not invent data, quotes, metrics, competitors, research findings, causes, or source claims. If the data is incomplete, biased, stale, or too small to support a conclusion, say so. For financial, legal, medical, or high-stakes claims, keep recommendations cautious and evidence-based.

## Final check

Before responding, verify that the output is specific, decision-useful, grounded in the provided inputs, and easy for a business user to act on.
