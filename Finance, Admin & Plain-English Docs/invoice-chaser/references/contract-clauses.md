# Contract Clauses — Payment Terms Reference

## When to Use This File

Read when the user needs help with:
- Adding late fee language to a contract or invoice
- Setting up deposit/retainer terms
- Drafting a payment terms section for a new agreement
- Adjusting payment terms after a chronic late-payer situation

---

## Late Fee Clause (Add to Contract)

### Standard (1.5% per month)
> "Invoices unpaid after [15/30] days from the invoice date will accrue a late payment
> fee of 1.5% per month (18% annually) on the outstanding balance. [Business Name]
> reserves the right to suspend services until all outstanding balances are paid in full."

### Flat fee alternative (simpler for smaller projects)
> "A late payment fee of $[amount] will be applied to any invoice unpaid after [N] days
> from the invoice date."

### Grace period version (softer, for ongoing relationships)
> "Payment is due within [30] days of the invoice date. A grace period of [5] business
> days applies. After the grace period, a late fee of 1.5% per month will be applied
> to any unpaid balance."

**Note on enforceability**: Late fee clauses are enforceable in most jurisdictions if
they are disclosed in advance (in the contract or on the invoice itself) and are not
"unconscionably" high. 1.5%/month (18% APR) is the widely accepted standard and is
legally safe in the US, UK, EU, and most other markets. Check local law for consumer
contracts specifically.

## Deposit / Upfront Payment Clause

### 50% deposit (most common for project work)
> "[Business Name] requires a non-refundable deposit of 50% of the total project fee
> before work commences. The remaining 50% is due upon project completion, prior to
> final file delivery. Work will not begin until the deposit invoice is paid."

### Milestone-based (for larger projects)
> "Payment is structured in milestones as follows:
> — 33% upon project kickoff
> — 33% upon [milestone, e.g., first draft / design approval]
> — 34% upon final delivery
> Each milestone payment is due within [7] days of the milestone being reached."

### Retainer (ongoing services)
> "[Client] agrees to pay a monthly retainer of $[amount] on the [1st/15th] of each
> month in advance of services rendered for that month. Services for a given month
> will not commence until the retainer for that month has been received."

## Net Terms (Payment Window) Options

| Term | What It Means | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Due on receipt | Pay immediately when invoice is sent | One-time clients, new relationships |
| Net-7 | Pay within 7 days | Short projects, milestone payments |
| Net-15 | Pay within 15 days | Standard freelance (increasingly common) |
| Net-30 | Pay within 30 days | Established relationships, larger agencies |
| Net-45/60 | Pay within 45–60 days | Corporate clients (their standard) |

**Recommendation for freelancers**: Net-15 is becoming the new standard. Net-30 was
borrowed from traditional B2B vendor relationships and is often unnecessarily long for
freelance/agency work where no goods are in transit.

## Payment Methods Clause

> "[Business Name] accepts payment via [bank transfer / ACH / SEPA / PayPal / Stripe /
> credit card / check]. International clients are responsible for any transfer fees.
> Payments must be made in [USD/GBP/EUR] unless otherwise agreed in writing."

## Suspension of Services Clause

> "[Business Name] reserves the right to suspend all services and withhold delivery of
> work product if any invoice remains unpaid for more than [15] days past its due date.
> Work will resume within [2] business days of full payment being received. [Business Name]
> is not liable for any project delays resulting from work suspension due to non-payment."

## Full Payment Terms Section (Plug-and-Play)

Use this as a complete Payment Terms section for a freelance or agency contract:

---

**PAYMENT TERMS**

Fees and payment schedule are as set out in the Project Proposal / Statement of Work
attached to this agreement.

**Invoicing**: [Business Name] will issue invoices as specified in the Project Proposal.
All invoices are payable within [15] days of the invoice date.

**Late Payment**: Invoices unpaid after [15] days from the invoice date will accrue a
late payment fee of 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance. [Business Name] reserves
the right to suspend all services until outstanding invoices are paid in full.

**Deposits**: A deposit of [50%] of the total project fee is required before work
commences. The deposit is non-refundable unless [Business Name] cancels the project
without cause.

**Expenses**: Pre-approved expenses will be invoiced separately and are payable within
[15] days of invoice. Receipts will be provided upon request.

**Disputes**: Any invoice dispute must be raised in writing within [7] days of receipt.
Undisputed portions of an invoice remain payable on the normal schedule.

## Invoice-Level Payment Terms (Shorter, for Invoice Footer)

> "Payment due: [date]. Late payments subject to 1.5%/month fee. Pay via: [link/details]."

> "Net 15. Invoice overdue after [date]. Late fee of 1.5%/month applies to unpaid balances."

## Raising Your Rates / Changing Terms Mid-Relationship

When moving a long-term client to new payment terms (shorter window, deposit requirement):

**Suggested email:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> As we head into [new year / new project phase], I'm updating my standard payment terms.
> Going forward, I'll be moving to Net-15 (from Net-30) and requiring a 50% deposit
> to kick off new projects.
>
> This is a change I'm making across all my client relationships — it's not a reflection
> of anything specific to our work together, which I really value.
>
> The updated terms will apply from [date / next project]. Happy to answer any questions.
>
> [Name]

Keep it matter-of-fact. Don't over-explain or apologize — this is a business decision.
Clients who push back hard on standard payment terms are often the chronic late payers
already causing you problems.
