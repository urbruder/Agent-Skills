# Statement of Work (SOW) — Full Template

Use this for: retainers, enterprise projects, agency engagements, or any project where both
parties need legal clarity and a paper trail. This is a document, not an email.

---

## SOW Structure

### Header Block
```
STATEMENT OF WORK

Project: [Project Name]
Client: [Legal Entity Name]
Contractor / Agency: [Your Legal Entity Name]
Date: [Issue Date]
SOW Version: 1.0
```

---

### 1. Project Overview
2–3 paragraphs:
- Business context: why this project exists and what it's meant to solve
- Engagement summary: what you'll deliver at the highest level
- Success definition: how both parties will know the project succeeded

---

### 2. Scope of Work

List every deliverable with specificity. Use sub-bullets for detail.

**In Scope:**
- [Deliverable 1]: [Specific description, format, quantity]
- [Deliverable 2]: [Specific description, format, quantity]
- [Deliverable 3]: [Specific description, format, quantity]

**Explicitly Out of Scope:**
State what is NOT included. This section prevents scope creep.
- [Thing client might assume is included]
- [Common extension that requires a separate agreement]
- [Ongoing work beyond the defined engagement]

---

### 3. Timeline & Milestones

| Milestone | Description | Due Date |
|---|---|---|
| Kickoff | [What happens] | [Date] |
| [Milestone 1] | [What's delivered] | [Date] |
| [Milestone 2] | [What's delivered] | [Date] |
| Final Delivery | [What constitutes completion] | [Date] |

**Timeline dependencies:**
Note any client responsibilities that affect your schedule:
- Client to provide [asset/access/approval] by [date] for [milestone] to be met
- [X] business days for client review at each milestone

---

### 4. Client Responsibilities

List explicitly what you need from them:
- Provide [assets, credentials, access] by [date]
- Designate one primary point of contact with authority to approve
- Provide feedback within [X] business days of each deliverable
- Make payment per schedule below

---

### 5. Revision Policy

State clearly:
- **Included rounds:** [Number] rounds of revisions included per deliverable
- **What counts as a revision:** Changes to scope, content, or direction after approval
- **Additional revisions:** Billed at $[rate]/hour beyond included rounds
- **Approval = sign-off:** Written approval (email is sufficient) locks that phase

#### Expenses

---

### 7. Intellectual Property

Choose one:

**Option A — Full transfer on payment:**
> Upon receipt of full payment, Contractor assigns all intellectual property rights in the
> deliverables to Client.

**Option B — License (retain underlying IP):**
> Contractor retains all rights to underlying tools, frameworks, and pre-existing IP.
> Client receives a perpetual, non-exclusive license to use the deliverables for their
> intended purpose.

**Both options should include:**
> Contractor retains the right to display the work in their portfolio unless Client
> requests otherwise in writing.

---

### 8. Confidentiality

Standard mutual NDA language or reference your standard NDA if separate.

---

### 9. Termination

**Termination for convenience:**
> Either party may terminate with [14/30] days written notice. Client owes all fees for
> work completed and in-progress. Deposit is non-refundable.

**Termination for cause:**
> If Client fails to pay within [X] days of due date, Contractor may pause or terminate
> the engagement. All delivered work reverts to Contractor until payment is received.

---

### 10. Limitation of Liability

> Contractor's total liability shall not exceed the fees paid under this SOW.
> Contractor is not liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.

*(Note: For large enterprise projects, have this reviewed by a lawyer.)*

## SOW Writing Tips

- **Every number should have a unit.** Not "3 revisions" — "3 rounds of revisions, defined as a consolidated set of changes submitted within [X] business days of delivery."
- **Define your terms.** If "deliverable" or "approval" appears, define what they mean in your context.
- **Milestone payments de-risk both sides.** Clients hate big deposits; milestones make it feel fair while keeping you protected.
- **The out-of-scope section is your best friend.** Write it last, after you've written everything in-scope. You'll see the gaps.
