# Deck Pitch — Live Presentation Structure

Use this when: pitching in person or on video, not sending a document. The deck is a prop for
your talk, not a document to be read. If someone could understand it without you presenting,
it has too many words.

---

## The Core Principle

A deck pitch is a performance. The slides are stage directions. You are the proposal.

**Each slide should hold one idea. One.**

---

## Slide Structure (10–12 slides)

### Slide 1 — THE TENSION (not "About Us")
Open with their problem, not your company.

Content: 1 sentence. Maybe an image. No bullet points.

Example: *"You're spending $40K/month on ads that convert at 1.2%."*

---

### Slide 2 — WHY THIS IS HARD
Validate that the problem is genuinely difficult. This builds credibility.

Content: 2–3 reasons the obvious solution doesn't work. Keep it short.
*(Shows you understand the nuance, not just the surface)*

---

### Slide 3 — OUR TAKE
Your point of view. This is the intellectual core of your pitch.

Content: Your named methodology, framework, or philosophy. Give it a name.
This is what differentiates you from "we'll do exactly what you asked."

Example: *"We call it the Conversion Architecture approach: fix the funnel before feeding it."*

---

### Slide 4 — THE PROOF
One case study. Similar client, similar problem, specific result.

Content:
- Client type (name if you have permission)
- The situation
- What you did
- The outcome — with a number

Not three case studies. One.

---

### Slide 5 — WHAT WE'D DO (Phase 1)

Content: Phase name + 2 sentences on outcome. NOT a task list.

---

### Slide 6 — WHAT WE'D DO (Phase 2)

Same format. Keep moving.

---

### Slide 7 — WHAT WE'D DO (Phase 3)

Same format. 3 phases maximum for a deck — details go in the written proposal.

---

### Slide 8 — WHAT YOU'LL GET

Simple list of deliverables. Bullets are fine here.

---

### Slide 9 — THE TEAM (if relevant)

Only include if team composition is a selling point. Keep it: photo, name, one-line credential.
Skip bios. No one reads them in a slide.

---

### Slide 10 — INVESTMENT

One number. Or two options named by outcome.

Do not put pricing in small text. Make it big. Own it.

Also show: timeline and payment structure.

---

### Slide 11 — QUESTIONS + NEXT STEPS

Not a "Thank You" slide. Close with action.

Content:
- "What questions do you have?"
- The one thing that needs to happen next
- A date or deadline

---

## Presenting Tips

**On the call:**
- Send the deck 10 minutes before, but tell them: *"I'll walk you through it — don't read ahead."*
- Pause after Slide 1. Let the tension land.
- Slow down on Slide 3 (your POV). This is where you earn trust.
- When you hit pricing, stop talking. Let them look. Silence is fine.

**Objection handling during the pitch:**
- "That's more than we planned" → "What were you expecting? I want to understand the gap."
- "Can you do it cheaper?" → "I can adjust scope. What would you remove?"
- "We're comparing a few options" → "That's fair. What's most important to you in who you choose?"
- "We need to think about it" → "Of course. What specifically is giving you pause?"

**The close:**
Don't end with "Let me know what you think." End with:
*"Based on everything we've discussed — does this feel like the right fit for what you're trying to do?"*

It's a yes/no question. Let them answer.
