# Cold DM Sequence Builder

Builds a 5-touch DM sequence for LinkedIn, Instagram, or other social platforms that feels human, gets replies, and books meetings — without triggering spam instincts or getting accounts flagged.

---

## Step 1: Extract Key Inputs

Before writing, gather (from context or by asking):

| Input | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Platform** | LinkedIn vs IG vs Twitter have very different norms and character limits |
| **Sender persona** | Founder? SDR? Recruiter? Freelancer? Tone shifts significantly |
| **Target persona** | Who exactly are they messaging? Title, industry, pain points |
| **Offer / CTA** | What's the ask? Discovery call? Demo? Free audit? Collab? |
| **Value prop** | One crisp sentence: what problem do you solve for this person? |
| **Tone** | Direct/formal vs casual/conversational |
| **Any past attempts** | If they have existing messages, review what's not working |

If the user gave you most of this, infer the rest and state your assumptions. Don't ask for everything if context makes it clear.

---

## Step 2: Sequence Architecture

Every sequence follows this 5-touch structure. Timing guidance is included — adjust for platform norms.

### Touch 1 — The Connection / First Contact
**Goal:** Get accepted / open the conversation. No pitch.  
**Timing:** Day 0  
**Rules:**
- Reference something real and specific (their post, a mutual connection, a company milestone, a piece of content)
- One sentence about who you are — only if relevant
- No ask. No pitch. Just open.
- LinkedIn: use connection note (300 chars) OR first message after connecting
- IG/Twitter: short, punchy, feels like a real human noticed them

### Touch 2 — The Soft Value Drop
**Goal:** Deliver something useful before asking for anything.  
**Timing:** Day 2–3 after connection  
**Rules:**
- Share a relevant insight, resource, observation, or compliment about their work
- Tease your relevance but don't sell
- End with a low-friction question (not "got 15 minutes?")
- Keep it under 4 sentences

### Touch 3 — The Relevance Bridge
**Goal:** Connect their world to your offer naturally.  
**Timing:** Day 5–7  
**Rules:**
- Reference something specific about their situation (role, company stage, content, pain point)
- Introduce what you do — one line, benefit-first
- Soft CTA: "Would it make sense to..." / "Open to a quick chat about..."
- Not a hard ask. A door.

### Touch 4 — The Social Proof Bump
**Goal:** Reduce perceived risk. Make saying yes easier.  
**Timing:** Day 10–12  
**Rules:**
- Name-drop a similar customer or result (without being braggy)
- Keep it contextual: "We helped [similar company] do X — thought it might be relevant for you"
- Restate the CTA simply
- If they've engaged at all (liked, replied briefly), reference it

### Touch 5 — The Graceful Exit
**Goal:** Last try. Leave the door open. Protect your reputation.  
**Timing:** Day 16–20  
**Rules:**
- Acknowledge this is the last nudge
- Make it easy to say no AND easy to say "not now"
- "If timing's off, totally fine — I'll leave it here" style
- Optional: offer a lower-commitment alternative (send a resource, answer a question)
- Never guilt-trip or pressure

## Step 3: Writing Rules (apply to every message)

### DO
- **Personalize the first line** — always. Generic openers get deleted.
- **Write like a human texted them** — short sentences, contractions, no corporate-speak
- **One idea per message** — don't stack multiple questions or pitches
- **Benefit-first language** — "helps you X" not "we offer Y"
- **Use their language** — mirror how they describe their work in their bio/posts
- **Be specific** — "saw your post on founder burnout last Tuesday" beats "love your content"

### DON'T
- Open with "I" (especially "I wanted to reach out...")
- Use hollow phrases: "Hope this finds you well", "I'd love to pick your brain", "circling back"
- Pitch in message 1. Ever.
- Use ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation marks, or emoji spam
- Write more than 5–7 sentences per message
- Ask multiple questions in one message
- Copy-paste the same message to 500 people (personalization tokens ≠ personalization)

---

## Step 4: Platform-Specific Adaptations

### LinkedIn
- Connection request note: ≤ 300 characters, no pitch, just reason for connecting
- Messages: professional but warm, not stiff
- Reference their posts, company news, shared groups/events
- Avoid: "I came across your profile" (everyone says this)

### Instagram
- Very short first DM — feels like a DM from a real follower
- Reference their content specifically (Reel, Story, post topic)
- Casual tone, use their aesthetic language
- Avoid: anything that sounds like copy-paste mass outreach

### Twitter/X
- Even shorter, wittier
- Can reference a tweet directly
- Reply to their tweet first (before DMing) for warm context

## Step 5: Output Format

Deliver the sequence as:

```
COLD DM SEQUENCE
Platform: [LinkedIn / Instagram / Twitter]
Target: [persona description]
Offer: [what you're pitching]
─────────────────────────────────

TOUCH 1 — Day 0: First Contact
[Message text]

TOUCH 2 — Day 2–3: Value Drop
[Message text]

TOUCH 3 — Day 5–7: Relevance Bridge
[Message text]

TOUCH 4 — Day 10–12: Social Proof
[Message text]

TOUCH 5 — Day 16–20: Graceful Exit
[Message text]

─────────────────────────────────
TIMING SUMMARY
[Brief table or list of send days]

PERSONALIZATION NOTES
[What the sender should customize per prospect — e.g., "Replace [their recent post] with a real post topic from their feed"]
```

---

## Step 6: Offer a Variant or Tweak

After delivering the sequence, offer:
- A **shorter 3-touch version** for low-volume, high-value prospects
- A **tone variant** (more direct / more casual)
- **Subject line equivalents** if they want to adapt for email
- **A/B test suggestion** for touch 1 if they're sending volume

---

## Red Flags to Call Out

If the user's context includes any of these, flag it:
- **Offer is too vague** — "let's connect and see how we can help each other" won't convert; help them sharpen it
- **Target is too broad** — "anyone in marketing" means the personalization won't land; push for a narrower ICP
- **CTA is too big** — asking for a 45-min demo as first ask will kill reply rates; suggest a smaller first step
- **No value prop** — if they can't state what problem they solve in one sentence, the sequence will read as fluff
