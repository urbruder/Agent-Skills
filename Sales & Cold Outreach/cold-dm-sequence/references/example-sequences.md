# Example Cold DM Sequences

## Example 1: B2B SaaS Founder → Head of Sales (LinkedIn)

**Sender:** Founder of a sales analytics tool  
**Target:** VP of Sales at a 150-person SaaS company  
**Offer:** Discovery call to demo pipeline forecasting product  
**Tone:** Direct, peer-to-peer

---

**TOUCH 1 — Day 0: First Contact**

> Your post about pipeline sandbaggers last week — spot on. That "committed but never closes" rep problem is something I hear from every VP I talk to. Sending a connect request — would love to follow your content.

*(LinkedIn connection note — 278 chars)*

---

**TOUCH 2 — Day 2–3: Value Drop**

> Hey [Name], saw you accepted — appreciate it.
>
> Pulled together a short breakdown of how 3 VPs we've spoken with tackled the sandbagging problem without destroying rep trust. Happy to send it over if useful — no strings, just thought it was relevant given your post.

---

**TOUCH 3 — Day 5–7: Relevance Bridge**

> [Name] — given you're scaling a team past 50 reps, forecast accuracy probably keeps you up at night more than quota attainment.
>
> We built a tool that flags at-risk deals 3 weeks earlier than CRM data alone — without reps having to change how they log. Used by a few teams your size at Series B–C.
>
> Would it make sense to jump on a 20-min call to see if it's even relevant for where you're at?

---

**TOUCH 4 — Day 10–12: Social Proof**

> Hey [Name] — just wanted to add some context. The VP of Sales at [Similar Company] was in the same boat — 60-rep team, forecast calls were basically guesswork. They got to 87% forecast accuracy in Q1 after using us for 8 weeks.
>
> If that kind of outcome would matter for your team, I'd love 20 mins. If not, totally fine — just didn't want to leave that out.

---

**TOUCH 5 — Day 16–20: Graceful Exit**

> [Name] — I'll leave it here after this one. I know inbound from strangers on LinkedIn is noise most days.
>
> If pipeline visibility ever becomes a burning priority and you want to chat, I'm easy to find. And if the timing's just off right now — totally understand.
>
> Either way, best of luck with the team this quarter.

## Example 2: Freelance Designer → E-commerce Founder (Instagram)

**Sender:** Brand identity designer  
**Target:** Founder of a growing DTC skincare brand  
**Offer:** Brand refresh / packaging redesign  
**Tone:** Warm, creative, casual

---

**TOUCH 1 — Day 0: First Contact**

> Your Reels showing the product formulation process are genuinely great — most brands never pull back the curtain like that. Started following.

---

**TOUCH 2 — Day 2–3: Value Drop**

> Hey [Name] — been following along for a bit. Noticed your packaging leans pretty minimal right now. I put together a quick mood board for a direction that might fit where your brand feels like it's heading — would you want me to send it? No pitch, just something I made while thinking about your aesthetic.

---

**TOUCH 3 — Day 5–7: Relevance Bridge**

> Sent that mood board idea a couple days ago — happy to share if you're curious.
>
> I do brand identity and packaging for DTC wellness brands at the Series A–B stage. Worked with [Brand Name] on their rebrand last year when they were about your size.
>
> If you're ever thinking about tightening up the visual identity as you scale, would love to chat — even just to share what I've seen work.

---

**TOUCH 4 — Day 10–12: Social Proof**

> [Name] — quick one. [Client Brand] came to me with similar vibes to yours — great product, founder-led story, but packaging that wasn't converting on shelf. After the rebrand, their conversion rate on the DTC site went up 22% in 90 days.
>
> Not saying that's guaranteed — just thought the parallel was worth mentioning. Happy to share the case study if you'd like.

---

**TOUCH 5 — Day 16–20: Graceful Exit**

> Hey [Name] — last message from me, promise.
>
> If a brand refresh isn't on the radar right now, completely get it — you've clearly got a lot going on. I'll stop popping up in your DMs.
>
> If it ever becomes relevant, you know where to find me. Rooting for the brand regardless — it's genuinely good.

## Example 3: Recruiter → Senior Engineer Passive Candidate (LinkedIn)

**Sender:** Technical recruiter at a growth-stage startup  
**Target:** Senior backend engineer, not actively looking  
**Offer:** Senior engineering role, strong comp + equity  
**Tone:** Respectful, low pressure, peer-level

---

**TOUCH 1 — Day 0: First Contact**

> Hi [Name] — your open-source work on [repo/project] came up in a conversation with our eng team this week. Really clean approach to [specific thing]. I recruit for engineering roles and wanted to connect — no pressure, just appreciate good work.

---

**TOUCH 2 — Day 2–3: Value Drop**

> Thanks for connecting. I know unsolicited recruiter messages are usually a slog, so I'll keep it brief.
>
> I'm working with a team building [one-sentence description — e.g., "real-time infrastructure for financial data at scale"]. The founding eng team has a few alumni from [recognizable companies] — people I thought you might find interesting to know.
>
> Not asking for anything right now — just curious if that kind of work is in the general direction you're interested in.

---

**TOUCH 3 — Day 5–7: Relevance Bridge**

> [Name] — based on your background in [their specialty], this role is probably either really interesting to you or completely irrelevant, so I'd rather just be direct.
>
> The company is pre-IPO, strong Series B, and the role would own [specific meaningful scope]. Comp is [$X–$Y] base + meaningful equity at this stage.
>
> If you're even 10% open to hearing more, I'm happy to share the full picture. If not, totally fine — I won't keep following up.

---

**TOUCH 4 — Day 10–12: Social Proof**

> Hey [Name] — last time I'll bring this up after today. Two engineers who joined this team in the past year came from similar backgrounds to yours. One told me it was the first role where he felt like he had real ownership over architecture decisions.
>
> If that sounds appealing even hypothetically, a 15-min call to hear more might be worth it. I'll do the pitch, you can say no — fair deal?

---

**TOUCH 5 — Day 16–20: Graceful Exit**

> [Name] — signing off on this one. I know the timing might just not be right, and that's completely valid.
>
> If anything changes — or if you ever want an intro to the founding team just to expand your network — feel free to reach back out. I'll keep this on my radar.
>
> Good luck with whatever you're building.
